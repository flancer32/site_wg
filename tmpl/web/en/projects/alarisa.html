Alarisa

Alarisa is a private digital counterpart for one person. It is being developed to help turn intent into coherent digital work — now with AI agents as practical capabilities, not as a replacement for judgment.

Digital life is scattered across messages, notes, files, services, and short-lived chats. Context disappears, priorities drift, and an agent that acts without enough understanding can easily do the wrong thing. Alarisa is intended to keep the useful context and continuity around a person’s real work, so that the next step starts from more than a blank prompt.

What it is meant to do

Alarisa will help clarify goals, keep related work connected over time, and prepare or delegate digital tasks through approved tools and agents. The person remains in charge: consequential actions should be visible, bounded, and confirmed when the authority or risk is unclear.

The project is built with my own TeqFW framework and ADSM methodology. They provide the engineering structure and living project context needed to develop an agent-based tool deliberately rather than as a collection of prompts.

Alarisa is still in development. If you are interested in trying it or following its progress, please find a way to reach me on the contacts page.
